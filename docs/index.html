Introduction

Tennis Lineup is a web app for USTA team captains to manage rosters and generate optimal match lineups. No installation required — it runs entirely in your browser and saves your roster locally.

Key Features

  • Roster management with manual entry or TennisRecord import
  • Player profiles with status badges (Hot, Improving, Caution, CPT)
  • Partnership tracking for doubles pairs with win/loss history
  • Automated lineup generation across 5 positions (S1, S2, D1, D2, D3)
Designed for: USTA Women's 4.0 teams. The optimizer uses dynamic ratings, court side preferences, and partnership records to build the strongest possible lineup for each match.

Quick Start

Get your first lineup in under two minutes:

  1. Open the app in any browser — no login or installation needed.
  2. Click + Add Player to create player profiles with name, dynamic rating, court side preference, and match type.
  3. Build out your full roster (the app comes pre-loaded with a base roster you can customize).
  4. Check the boxes for players who are available for the upcoming match. A minimum of 8 is recommended for a complete lineup.
  5. Enter opponent details in Step 2 — use Full Scouting for rated matchup analysis, or Quick Mode to skip opponent info.
  6. Tap Generate Optimal Lineup and review the result.

Adding Players

Click + Add Player in the roster section to open the add panel. There are two ways to create a player profile:

Method 1 — Name or TennisRecord Lookup

Type a player's name (e.g. Jane Smith) or paste their full TennisRecord URL into the lookup field, then click Lookup. The app will auto-fill the player's name and current dynamic rating from TennisRecord.

Managing Availability

Use Select All and Clear to quickly toggle the full roster. Individual players can be checked or unchecked by tapping their card.

Partnerships

Tracking doubles pairings improves lineup accuracy. Click Partnerships to record who plays well together.

  1. Select Player A from the dropdown.
  2. Select Player B from the dropdown.
  3. Enter Wins Together and Losses Together.
  4. Set the Trend: Trending Up, Stable, or Trending Down.
  5. Add optional Notes (chemistry, scheduling, injury history).
  6. Click Save Partnership.

How Trends Affect the Lineup

  • Trending Up — pair gets priority placement in higher doubles slots (D1, D2)
  • Stable — placed based on combined rating and win percentage
  • Trending Down — the optimizer may suggest splitting the pair
Tip: Undefeated partnerships are flagged with a green checkmark in the results. The optimizer will protect these pairs unless availability forces a change.

Generating a Lineup

  1. Mark which players are available for the match (Step 1 on the main page).
  2. Enter opponent information in Step 2 — or switch to Quick Mode to skip this.
  3. Tap Generate Optimal Lineup.
  4. Review the recommended lineup, matchup analysis, and confidence ratings. Scroll down for alternative lineups.

What the Algorithm Considers

  • Dynamic ratings and 4.0 win percentages
  • Court side preferences (Ad / Deuce / Flexible)
  • Primary line designation (Singles-only, Doubles-only, Both)
  • Doubles partnership records and trend status
  • Opponent ratings (in Full Scouting mode)

Quick Mode

If you don't have opponent information, toggle Quick (No Opponent Info) at the top of Step 2. The optimizer will build the strongest internal lineup without opponent matchup analysis.

Minimum requirement: 8 available players for a complete lineup (2 singles players + 3 doubles pairs).

Line Positions

Each match has 5 lines — 2 singles and 3 doubles pairs. The optimizer assigns players to lines based on strength, court side, and availability.

LineTypeDescription
S1Singles 1Your strongest singles player, placed against the opponent's top singles player.
S2Singles 2Second-ranked singles competitor.
D1Doubles 1Your top doubles pair, matched against the opponent's strongest doubles team.
D2Doubles 2Mid-tier partnership.
D3Doubles 3Strategic placement — often used to secure a win with a favorable matchup.

Court Sides

  • Ad — left court (advantage side)
  • Deuce — right court
  • Flexible — can play either side; the optimizer places them where they're most useful

Line Preference

A player's Primary Line setting restricts which positions they're eligible for. A Singles-only player will never be placed in doubles, and a Doubles-only player will never be placed in singles. Players set to Both can fill any slot.

Opponent Scouting

Step 2 lets you enter the opposing team's lineup. The more information you provide, the more precise the matchup analysis.

Full Scouting Mode

Enter names and ratings for all 8 opponent positions (S1, S2, D1–D3). For each player you can:

  • Type their name and click 🔍 to auto-fetch their dynamic rating from TennisRecord
  • Click 🔗 to manually enter a TennisRecord profile URL
  • Enter the rating directly in the rating field

Quick Mode

Toggle Quick (No Opponent Info) at the top of Step 2 to skip scouting and generate a lineup based only on your team's ratings and partnerships.

Paste Opponent Roster

Click Paste Opponent Roster to bulk-fill the lineup from copied text. Supported formats:

  • S1: Jane Smith 3.75 — name + rating with position label
  • D1: Alice Brown 3.68 / Eve Wilson 3.65 — doubles pair
  • Raw TennisRecord URLs (ratings fetched automatically)
Name Lookup: Type any opponent's name in a name field, then click the 🔍 button to instantly look up their current dynamic rating on TennisRecord — no URL needed.

Player Ratings

The app uses USTA dynamic ratings — a continuously updated decimal number that reflects recent match performance.

Rating Scale (4.0 Level)

  • 3.85 – 4.00 — top of the 4.0 tier, near bump territory
  • 3.65 – 3.84 — solidly mid-4.0
  • 3.50 – 3.64 — lower 4.0, may be coming up from 3.5

Status Badges

Each player card shows a status badge based on recent trends:

Hot Winning streak — performing above their rating

Improving Upward trend — rating is rising

Caution Recent losses — may be fatigued or underperforming

CPT Team captain

How Ratings Are Used

The optimizer combines the dynamic rating with a player's 4.0 Win % (how often they win against 4.0-level opponents) to rank players for each position. In Full Scouting mode, individual matchup edges are calculated and shown in the results.

Updating Ratings

To update a player's rating, open their card and edit the Dynamic Rating field, or use the TennisRecord lookup in the Add Player panel to re-fetch the latest value.
