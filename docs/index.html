Rafael RC Lineup Optimizer

A smart lineup tool for the Rafael RC 4.0 Women's team. It uses USTA dynamic ratings, win/loss records, partnership history, and opponent scouting to recommend the optimal lineup for each match.

ⓘ
Live app: laurenvolpi-crypto.github.io/tennnis-lineup1 — works on any phone or desktop, no install needed.

What it does

The optimizer evaluates thousands of possible lineup combinations and picks the one most likely to win the most lines. It accounts for:

  • USTA dynamic ratings — the official estimated rating for each player
  • Win/loss records — 2025 and 2026 Women's 3.5 + 4.0 adult matches (no Combo or Mixed)
  • Partnership history — how pairs have actually performed together
  • Court side preference — Ad side vs. Deuce side fit
  • Opponent scouting — optional ratings for each opponent player
  • Singles capability — only players flagged as singles-capable are placed in singles lines

Format it's designed for

USTA 4.0 Women's Adult format: 2 singles lines (S1, S2) and 3 doubles lines (D1, D2, D3). Eight players are placed total — 2 in singles and 6 across three doubles pairs.

🎯 How to Use the App

1
Mark who's available
All players are selected by default. Tap any player to toggle them unavailable (grayed out with a dashed border). Only available players are considered in the lineup.
2
Enter the opponent lineup (optional)
Switch to Full Scouting mode and fill in opponent names and ratings. You can paste a roster all at once using Paste Opponent Roster. Or use Quick Mode to skip opponent info entirely.
3
Tap Generate Optimal Lineup
The optimizer runs and shows the top recommendation plus up to 3 alternatives. Each line includes a confidence rating, rating-edge indicator, and reasoning notes.
4
Save or print
Tap Save / Print Lineup at the bottom of the results. On mobile, use your browser's share sheet to save as PDF or screenshot.
✨
Tip: You need at least 8 available players to generate a lineup. The Generate button is disabled until that threshold is met.

👥 Building Your Roster

The roster comes pre-loaded with all 27 Rafael RC players, sorted by rating. You can add temporary or sub players at any time.

Adding a player

Tap + Add Player. The fastest way is to paste a TennisRecord.com profile URL — the app fetches the player's name and dynamic rating automatically. Then fill in:

FieldWhat to enter
Dynamic RatingAuto-filled from TennisRecord. Otherwise enter their USTA estimated rating (e.g. 3.72).
Preferred SideAd (left court), Deuce (right court), or Flexible. Used for side-fit scoring.
Primary LineDoubles, Singles, or Both. Only players set to Singles or Both appear as singles candidates.
Win % estimateYour best guess at their win rate at 4.0 level, 0–100. Used as a tiebreaker.
2026 Wins / LossesCurrent-season record. Shown on the player card and used in scoring.

Removing a player

Tap the small × button in the top-right corner of any player card. Built-in players cannot be permanently removed this way — they'll come back on reload. To permanently remove a built-in player, edit the BASE_ROSTER array in index.html.

Tapping a player name

Tapping the player's name (or the ⓘ icon) opens a player profile sheet showing:

  • Full season records for 2025 and 2026
  • Partnership history with win/loss records and trend
  • Player patterns — form, court side, singles suitability, avoids
⚠
Added players are saved in your browser's local storage. They'll persist across sessions on the same device, but won't appear for other teammates unless the base roster is updated in the code.

🤝 Partnerships

Partnership data is one of the most powerful inputs to the optimizer. A pair with a strong track record gets a significant scoring bonus; a flagged "avoid" pair gets heavily penalized.

Viewing partnerships

Tap Partnerships to open the panel. All known partnerships — both built-in and any you've added — are listed at the bottom of the panel.

Adding or updating a partnership

Select Player A and Player B, enter their combined wins and losses, pick a trend, and add any notes. If the pair already exists, saving replaces the existing entry.

Built-in avoid pairs

Some pairs have been flagged as incompatible based on match history. The app currently avoids:

  • McGeehan + Klein — 0-2 together, cannot close matches
  • McGeehan + Volpi — 0-1, style mismatch
  • Liu + anyone except Rice — 1-8 without Rice at 4.0

These are coded in the AVOID array in index.html and can be edited directly.

✨
Captain tip: After each match, update the relevant partnership records so the optimizer learns from your team's actual results.

🔍 Opponent Lineup

Full Scouting vs. Quick Mode

Full Scouting lets you enter the opponent's names and ratings for each line. The optimizer then matches your players against their specific opponents to maximize rating edges. Quick Mode skips the opponent section entirely and optimizes based on your team's internal strengths only.

Pasting a roster

Tap Paste Opponent Roster and paste in any format. The parser handles all of these:

  • Plain list: Jane Smith 3.80 — sorted by rating and assigned to lines automatically
  • Labeled lines: S1: Alice Brown 3.75 or D1: Carol Davis 3.68 / Eve Wilson 3.65
  • TennisRecord URLs — ratings are fetched automatically

Mixed formats in a single paste are supported. URL-fetched players fill any remaining open slots.

Adding a TennisRecord link per opponent

Tap the 🔗 icon next to any opponent slot to expand a URL field. Entering a link makes the opponent's name in the results a clickable link to their full profile.

⚡ Generating a Lineup

Once 8+ players are marked available, the Generate Optimal Lineup button activates. The optimizer evaluates up to thousands of combinations and returns the top 5. Results appear below the button and the page scrolls automatically.

Minimum requirements

  • At least 8 available players
  • At least 2 players with Primary Line set to Singles or Both — these fill the S1 and S2 spots
⚠
If no singles-capable players are available, the Generate button will show a message asking you to mark at least 2 players as singles-eligible before retrying.

Alternative lineups

Up to 3 alternative lineups appear below the main result. Each shows how many optimizer points it scores below the top choice. These are useful when the captain has a gut feeling or availability changes last-minute.

📈 Confidence Scores

Every line and the overall match header shows a confidence indicator. This is a calculated estimate — not a guarantee — based on rating gaps and win/loss history.

Favorable (65%+)
Competitive (45–64%)
Tough (<45%)

Singles line confidence

Calculated from: 50 + (rating edge × 80) + (win% × 30), clamped to 20–90%.

A player rated 0.15 higher than their opponent with a 60% win rate would score: 50 + 12 + 18 = 80% → Favorable.

Doubles line confidence

Calculated from: 50 + (rating edge × 60) + (partnership win% × 25), with an additional −30 penalty if the pair has an avoid flag.

Overall match confidence

Derived from the optimizer's total score for the best lineup, normalized against a reference maximum of 500 points. It reflects how good the overall lineup is relative to what a hypothetical perfect matchup would score — not a win probability.

⚠
Confidence scores are most meaningful in Full Scouting mode when opponent ratings are entered. In Quick Mode, ratings default to 3.70 for all opponents, so the confidence primarily reflects your team's internal strengths.

⚙ How the Optimizer Works

The optimizer uses a brute-force combinatorial search over all valid lineups, scoring each one and returning the top 5. It does not use machine learning — every score is deterministic and explainable.

Step-by-step process

1
Identify singles candidates
Players with singlesCapable: true (line set to Singles or Both) are sorted by their singlesScore. Only the top 4–6 are tried as S1/S2 to keep combinations manageable.
2
Build the doubles pool
The remaining available players (after removing the two singles picks) are sorted by rating × (0.5 + winPct). The top 12 are kept as the doubles pool to cap computation time.
3
Generate all 6-player combinations
All combinations of 6 players from the doubles pool are generated. If the pool has exactly 6, there's only one combination. For 7 players there are 7 combos; for 12 there are 924.
4
Generate all pairings and line assignments
For each 6-player group, all ways to form 3 pairs are enumerated (15 pairings), and all 6 ways to assign those pairs to D1/D2/D3 are tried (90 per group).
5
Score every lineup
Each complete lineup (S1 + S2 + D1 + D2 + D3) is scored by summing the five line scores. The highest-scoring lineup is recommended.

🎯 Scoring Breakdown

Singles line score

Starting from a player's base singlesScore (set when the roster is built), the algorithm adds:

  • (player rating − opponent rating) × 40 — rating advantage
  • win% × 15 — weighted recent form bonus
  • −50 penalty if the player is Meredith Pavia (high-risk flag)

Doubles partnership score

The partnership score is the foundation of doubles placement. Each component:

FactorPoints
Avoid pair penalty−120
Partnership win % × 500 – 50
Sample size bonus (capped at 8 matches × 3)0 – 24
Trending up+12
Trending down−8
Perfect side fit (Ad + Deuce)+18
One flexible player+12
Both players same preferred side−5
(Average rating − 3.5) × 30varies
(Average win%) × 200 – 20
Pavia involvement penalty−60

Doubles line score vs. opponent

score = partnership_score + (team_avg_rating − opp_avg_rating) × 35

This means a pair with great chemistry can overcome a small rating deficit — and a high-rated pair with a poor track record may score lower than expected.

ⓘ
The optimizer does not "lock" your best pair into D1. It tries every assignment across D1, D2, D3. Sometimes it's strategically better to place your strongest pair at D2 to exploit a favorable matchup there.

📄 update_records.py

update_records.py is a Python script that automatically refreshes player data in index.html by scraping TennisRecord.com. It is designed to run every Friday before the weekend match.

What it updates

  • 2025 and 2026 Adult W/L records (Women's 3.5 + 4.0 only — Combo and Mixed excluded)
  • USTA Estimated Dynamic Rating for each player
  • Calculated win% (winPct40)
  • Performance tags (Hot, Caution, Trending Up) based on fresh data
  • Singles reasoning text for Hayley Burner and Meredith Pavia
  • Partnership trending fields recalculated from W/L ratios

Running the script

# Install dependencies (first time only)
pip install requests beautifulsoup4

# Run from inside the tennis-lineup folder
python update_records.py

The script edits index.html in place and then commits and pushes to GitHub automatically. Requires that git is configured with push access to the repo.

Adding a new player to the script

Find the PLAYER_URLS dictionary near the top of the script and add an entry:

"smith": "https://www.tennisrecord.com/adult/profile.aspx?playername=Jane%20Smith",

The key (e.g. "smith") must match the player's id field in the BASE_ROSTER array inside index.html.

Disambiguating players with common names

TennisRecord shows multiple profiles for common names. To target the right person, add an &s=N parameter where N is the section number shown in the URL when you navigate to the correct profile manually:

"hoffman": "https://www.tennisrecord.com/adult/profile.aspx?playername=Sarah%20Hoffman&s=2",
⚠
The script uses the allorigins.win proxy to avoid CORS restrictions. If TennisRecord changes its page structure, the regex patterns in the script may need updating.

✎ Manual Updates

To update a single player's data without running the full script, open index.html in a text editor and find the player's entry in the BASE_ROSTER array. Each player looks like this:

{ id:"volpi", name:"Lauren Volpi", rating:3.71, side:"ad", line:"doubles",
  singlesCapable:false, captain:false,
  record25:{w:19,l:12,note:"W 3.5+4.0"},
  record26:{w:7,l:6,note:"W 3.5+4.0"},
  winPct40:0.59, tags:[], singlesScore:30 },
FieldHow to update
ratingReplace with the new dynamic rating from TennisRecord
record26.w / record26.lUpdate after each match weekend
winPct40Recalculate: (w25+w26) / (total25+total26), round to 2 decimal places
side"ad", "deuce", or "both"
line"singles", "doubles", or "both"
singlesCapabletrue if line is singles or both, otherwise false
tagsArray of strings — e.g. ["Hot"], ["Caution"], ["Captain"]

Deploying changes

After editing index.html, commit and push to GitHub:

git add index.html
git commit -m "Update player records"
git push

GitHub Pages deploys automatically within ~60 seconds.

❓ FAQ & Captain Tips

The Generate button is grayed out — why?
You need at least 8 available players selected. Check the count below the roster grid. If it says "need at least 8," tap more players to mark them available.
The lineup puts a weak player at D1 — should I trust that?
Yes, sometimes. The optimizer assigns pairs to lines based on opponent matchup, not just raw strength. If the weakest opponent is at D1, putting a mid-tier but proven pair there while saving your strongest for D2 can be the right call strategically.
How do I override a recommendation?
The app is advisory only — use the alternatives section for the next-best options, or just ignore the recommendation. There's no "lock" feature; the captain always decides.
A player's rating looks wrong. How do I fix it?
Run update_records.py to pull the latest data from TennisRecord, or manually edit the rating field in BASE_ROSTER in index.html and push to GitHub.
Can I use this for a different USTA team?
Yes — the generic version at /Users/laurenvolpi/Claude/tennis-lineup-generic/index.html starts with an empty roster and no team-specific data. Use the settings panel (gear icon) to configure your team name and level.
Does the app store anything on a server?
No. Everything runs in your browser. Added players and custom partnerships are saved to localStorage on your device only — nothing is sent to any server.
What's the best time to run the update script?
Friday morning before the weekend match gives you the most current data. USTA typically processes match results within 1–3 days of play.
Why does the algorithm cap the doubles pool at 12 players?
Combinatorial explosion. With 12 players in the pool, generating all 6-player combinations is fast (<1 second). With 15+ players it can take several seconds or freeze on older phones. The top 12 by rating×win% are almost always the optimal group anyway.
